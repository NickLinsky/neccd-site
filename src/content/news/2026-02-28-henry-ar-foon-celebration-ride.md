Last October, over 80 riders gathered at Wompatuck State Park to celebrate the life and legacy of **Henry Ar Foon**, a beloved member of the NECCD community who passed away earlier in the year.

Henry was one of those people who made every ride feel like a party. He had a gift for making newcomers feel welcome, for cracking exactly the right joke at the top of a hard climb, and for remembering everyone's name. He rode with NECCD from our earliest days, and his presence shaped the kind of community we've become.

## The ride

The Henry Ar Foon Celebration Ride wound through trails and roads Henry loved — from Wompatuck State Park through Cohasset and along the coast. It was a glorious fall day, the kind Henry would have loved.

At the halfway point, we stopped for a moment of reflection. Friends and family shared memories — his joy on a bike, his commitment to welcoming riders of color into spaces they'd often felt excluded from, his laugh.

"Henry would have wanted us to have fun today," said his sister, Maya. "And I think we did."

## A photo gallery

A full gallery of photos from the ride is available in our Band community. Thank you to everyone who came, contributed photos, and helped organize.

## Henry's legacy

Henry's family has established a small fund in his name to support loaner bikes at NECCD events, so that no one ever has to miss a ride because they don't have a bike. We'll share more details soon about how to contribute.

Rest well, Henry. We'll keep riding.
