Last Tuesday, NECCD members testified before the Joint Committee on Transportation at the Massachusetts State House in support of **House Bill 3412**, which would require protected cycling infrastructure on all state-funded road reconstruction projects.

## The bill

HB 3412 — known informally as the "Complete Streets" bill — would mandate that any road project receiving state transportation funding include dedicated, protected cycling infrastructure wherever feasible. The bill is co-sponsored by 34 legislators and has received support from cycling advocates, public health groups, and transportation equity organizations across the state.

## NECCD's testimony

NECCD member Ayana Williams delivered testimony on behalf of the coalition, highlighting the safety disparities faced by cyclists of color in underinvested neighborhoods.

"Protected bike lanes aren't just a cycling issue," she said. "They're a racial justice issue, a public health issue, and a climate issue — and the communities that need them most are the ones that have been waiting the longest."

Three other NECCD members also submitted written testimony sharing their personal experiences cycling in Boston and Greater Boston.

## What's next

The bill passed the committee with a favorable recommendation and is now headed to a full floor vote, expected in May. NECCD will be organizing a presence at the State House on the day of the vote — watch our Band community and events calendar for details.
