Cycling advocacy is one of NECCD's core commitments. Join us for a breakfast ride to Cambridge City Hall, where we'll meet with local officials to discuss cycling equity, protected infrastructure, and what our communities need.

## Why this matters

Cyclists in lower-income neighborhoods and communities of color face disproportionate risks on the road — fewer protected lanes, worse road conditions, and less investment in cycling infrastructure. This ride is an opportunity to bring those voices directly to decision-makers.

## The morning

We'll meet at 8:00 AM at Cambridge City Hall plaza. After introductions and a light breakfast, we'll ride a short loop through some of the streets we're advocating for. At 10:00 AM, we'll return for a 45-minute meeting with city councilors and transportation staff.

You don't need to be an experienced advocate to come — just bring your experience as a cyclist and your voice.

## Details

- **Breakfast:** Coffee, pastries, and fruit will be provided
- **The ride:** ~4 miles, easy pace, flat terrain
- **The meeting:** Open to all attendees; no speaking required
- **What to wear:** Whatever you're comfortable riding in
