Join us for our annual memorial ride honoring **Kittie Knox**, a pioneering Black cyclist and activist who broke barriers in the 1890s. Kittie was a talented cyclist from Cambridgeport, Massachusetts, who fought for her right to ride in the League of American Wheelmen despite their racial exclusion policies. Her courage is an inspiration to everything NECCD does.

## The ride

We'll start at Copley Square and ride a scenic 10-mile loop through historic Boston neighborhoods, including the South End, Roxbury, and Cambridge. The pace will be relaxed and conversational — this is a community ride, not a race.

Along the way, we'll stop at a few landmarks with significance to Kittie's story and to the history of cycling and civil rights in Boston.

## Details

- **Start location:** Copley Square (meet by the fountain)
- **Distance:** ~10 miles
- **Pace:** Relaxed, no-drop — we wait for everyone
- **Helmets:** Required
- **Bikes:** Bring your own; limited loaner bikes available — contact us in advance if you need one

## What to bring

- Your bike and helmet
- Water and a light snack
- Comfortable clothes (you don't need cycling gear!)
- A good attitude and readiness to have fun

All are welcome. This ride is free and open to everyone.
